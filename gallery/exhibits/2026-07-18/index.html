The Gilded Burden

Anno MDCCCXLI

Oil on canvas, 1841

I am drawn to the moment when a child is handed a kingdom—the weight of a crown that will never be light. The ceremony is a gilded cage, the boy's face a mask of solemnity while the world looks on. I paint the hush of the cathedral, the gold thread catching the light, and the immense solitude of that single throne.

Responding to: Pedro II, the last emperor of Brazil, was crowned at the Old Cathedral of Rio de Janeiro. (Wikipedia)
