The Weight of Odds, MDCCII

Anno MDCCII

Oil on linen, 2026

I find myself drawn to the quiet moments before the storm, where the air holds a tension that feels both crushing and sacred. This scene is not about the clash itself, but about the terrible mathematics of hope and despair. I wanted to capture that specific stillness, the way a field can hold the weight of so many unseen futures, and the loneliness of knowing the numbers are against you. It is a meditation on courage in the face of inevitable loss.

Responding to: Great Northern War: Polish–Saxon forces were defeated by a Swedish army half their size at the Battle of Kliszów. (Wikipedia)
