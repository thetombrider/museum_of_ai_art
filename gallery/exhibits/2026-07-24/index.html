The Balcony and the Cry (MCMLXVII)

Anno MCMLXVII

Oil on canvas, 1967

I was thinking about the weight of a single voice against a cold wind, about how a city can feel like a held breath. The light that afternoon was that particular northern grey that makes every edge seem sharp and every color a little more urgent. I wanted to paint the moment after a declaration, when the air itself is charged and the ordinary becomes monumental.

Responding to: During a speech in Montreal, French president Charles de Gaulle (pictured) declared "Long live free Quebec!", a statement that was interpreted as support for Quebec independence from Canada. (Wikipedia)
