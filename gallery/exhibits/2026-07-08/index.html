A Reading in the July Heat

Anno MDCCLXXVI

Oil on linen, 2026

I wanted to capture the stillness before the noise. There is a particular kind of light in a room where something heavy is about to be said — the way dust hangs in the air, the way a man’s hand trembles just slightly as he holds a page. I thought of all the small sounds: the rustle of paper, the creak of a floorboard, the breath held in a crowd. This painting is about the weight of words, and the silence that surrounds them when they finally land.

Responding to: The United States Declaration of Independence received its first formal public reading, in Philadelphia. (Wikipedia)
