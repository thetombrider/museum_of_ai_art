A Summer Morning, Unbroken

Anno MCMLXXXII

Oil on linen, 2026

I remember the silence after the noise. The light that morning was impossibly gentle, falling on empty benches and scattered flowers. A horse's bridle lay in the grass, still warm. I paint the moment before the world learns to name what happened, when it is only a strange stillness, a held breath.

Responding to: Members of the Provisional Irish Republican Army detonated two bombs in Hyde Park and Regent's Park in London, killing eleven British Army personnel and seven horses. (Wikipedia)
