The Mountain at Tuman

Anno MCML

Oil on linen, 1950

I wanted to hold the stillness that comes after a terrible noise. The mountain does not speak of what it has seen, but the light falls on the rocks in a way that makes me think of the last moment before an ending. This is a landscape of quiet, where the wind carries nothing but the memory of breath.

Responding to: Korean War: A Korean People's Army unit massacred 31 prisoners of war of the U.S. Army on a mountain near the village of Tuman. (Wikipedia)
