The Woodland Retreat

Anno MDCCLXXVII

Oil on linen, 2026

I wanted to capture the moment when the forest itself seemed to hold its breath. The men moved through the trees not as soldiers but as shadows, each step a negotiation with the earth. There is a quiet dignity in retreat, a weight that settles on the shoulders like the damp air before rain. I painted the light filtering through the canopy as if it were the last kindness the day could offer.

Responding to: American Revolutionary War: British forces caught up with American troops withdrawing from Ticonderoga, capturing more than 200 men at the Battle of Hubbardton. (Wikipedia)
